"The Great Gatsby"
by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Set in the extravagant Roaring Twenties, this book delves into the life of enigmatic millionaire Jay Gatsby. It explores themes of wealth, the American Dream, and the illusion of happiness.


"1984"
by George Orwell

A dystopian classic, this book depicts a totalitarian society ruled by Big Brother. It examines themes of government surveillance, censorship, and the suppression of individuality.


"To the Lighthouse"
by Virginia Woolf

Set across different time periods, this introspective novel explores the lives and thoughts of the Ramsay family. It delves into themes of time, memory, and the essence of human experience.


"Pride and Prejudice"
by Jane Austen

This beloved novel follows the Bennet sisters as they navigate the social expectations and romantic entanglements of 19th-century England. It explores themes such as love, marriage, and societal expectations.


"The Catcher in the Rye"
by J.D. Salinger

Narrated by a disillusioned teenager named Holden Caulfield, this novel is a coming-of-age story that touches on themes of teenage angst, identity, and societal conformity.
